Instructions

In its current structure, 32 national teams compete inthe World Cup for approximately one month in the host country or countries.
The World Cup is divided into two stages,the group stage and the knockout stage. In the group stage, the teams are divided into eight houses of four teams each. The eight best teams are defined as head of house, and one of them, even if it is not suitable in terms of its achievements,
is always the team of the host country.
Each of the houses is run according to the "everyone against everyone" method, so that each team plays at least three times.
The first two teams in each house, in terms of the number of points, advance to the knockout stage.
In the knockout phase, each team faces its opponent only once, with the loser being eliminated from the tournament.
In case the game ends in a tie, it is extended by half an hour, and if the result remains the same, the decision will be made through a penalty shootout The knockout stage begins in the round of 16, where the group winners face the runners-up. After that, the quarter-finals, the semi-finals, the match for third place and the final were held. The winner of this game is crowned as the world soccer champion.
